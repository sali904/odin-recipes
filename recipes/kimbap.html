Extraordinary Attorney Woo's Kimbap

Attorney Woo's Favorite Food: Kimbap

Extraordinary Attorney Woo is a Korean legal drama that chronicles the life of an autistic lawyer, Woo Young-woo, as she navigates working at a top law firm. She often faces challenges due to the very nature of working in an ableist, patriarchal society as an autistic woman. However, she still works hard to help others through her job as a lawyer and, as the series progresses, to further understand herself and the world around her.

One distinctive characteristic of Woo Young-woo is her love for kimbap - a rice and seaweed roll that is often filled with a savory mix of protein and vegetables. Early in the show, Young-woo mentions that she always has "Woo Young-Woo kimbap" for breakfast (although she is shown eating a sandwich-like variation of kimbap made by her best friend Geu-rami for breakfast in another episode). "Woo Young-Woo kimbap" is a variation of classic kimbap - which consists of pickled radish, fish cake, carrots, spinach, eggs, and beef strips. However, instead of beef, Young-Woo's kimbap has ham in its place and also features crab sticks and braised burdock root. The healthy and varied fillings make kimbap, whether it is in it's classic form or varied like Young-woo's or Geu-rami's kimbap, an excellent snack or meal to enjoy at any time and place. As Young-woo states in the show: "Kimbap is reliable".

This recipe, courtesy of Korean Bapsang, may seem a bit intimidating to prepare at first, but trust me - it is actually pretty easy to prepare and so yummy. Assemble each component of the kimbap separately to make assembling the kimbap a breeze. When you're done, turn on an episode of Extraordinary Attorney Woo and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Ham
  • Crab sticks
  • Nori
  • Short-grain rice
  • Sesame seeds - for garnish
  • Sesame oil

To prepare the vegetables

  • Carrots
  • Spinach
  • Burdock root - can be store-bought
  • Yellow pickled radish
  • Sesame oil
  • Salt to taste

To prepare the fish cake

  • Fish cake
  • Soy sauce
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Prepare the rice using a 2:1 ratio of water to rice.
  2. Slice the ham into thin strips.
  3. Pan-fry the ham over medium-high heat until lightly browned.
  4. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Once water is boiling, blanch spincach and then immediately place in ice water to stop the cooking process. Squeeze the water out, then season with sesame oil and salt. Set aside.
  5. Julienne carrots. Lightly oil a pan and heat it over medium-high heat. Stir-fry carrots until soft, then season slightly with salt. Set aside.
  6. Cut the pickled radish lengthwise into 1/2 inch strips.
  7. Slice braised burdock root into vertical strips. Set aside.
  8. Beat eggs in bowl with a pinch of salt. Heat a lightly oiled or nonstick pan over medium-low heat. Add eggs to pan and flip once the bottom is cooked. Once both sides are cooked, tranfer eggs to a plate or cutting board and slice lengthwise into 3/4-inch thick stips. Set aside.
  9. Slice fish cake into 1/2 inch strips. Stir-fry in pan over medium heat with soy sauce,sugar, and sesame oil mixture. Remove from pan and set aside.
  10. Slice crab sticks into 1/2 inch strips. Set aside.
  11. Add sesame oil and salt to rice once it is finished cooking and mix well, allowing the rice to cool down while gaining flavor.
  12. Put seaweed sheet shiny side down on cutting board or bamboo mat. Spread about 3/4 cup to 1 cup of rice evenly over sheet.
  13. Lay prepared ingredients on top of rice.
  14. Roll rice and ingredients by lifting the bottom edge of the sheet and rolling it away from you using even presure to make sure everything is tucked in tightly.
  15. Rub or brush sesame oil on the roll for extra flavor and shine.
  16. Add a bit of sesame oil to a sharp knife and cut rolls into 1/2 inch sizes. Wipe knife with damp towl and add more sesame oil to knife if rice sticks.
